Bruschetta Recipe

Description

Bruschetta (pronounced brew-SKET-tah) is an Italian appetizer or antipasti that starts with a base of toasted or grilled bread. The bread is then served warm either with a topping spooned over or with a topping on the side so each person can build their own bruschetta. Bruschetta is meant to be served as a hand-held finger food, large enough for one or two bites.

Ingredients

  • 2 to 3 tomatoes (note that tomatoes must be ripe and fresh)
  • 1 clove of minced garlic
  • Â¼ bowl of olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon Balsamic Vinegar
  • 1 pinch of thyme (optional)
  • A little salt
  • A little pepper
  • 1 baguette

Steps

  1. Peel the tomato
    • An easy way to peel tomatoes is to briefly boil them.
    • Cut an X on the bottom of the tomato, this will help you peel them more conveniently.
    • Note, tomatoes are only allowed to boil briefly, that is, when the water is boiling, turn off the heat and put the tomatoes in for 1-2 minutes.
    • If you still want to try it longer, maybe the broth will turn into tomato soup.
  2. Heat the oven
    • Turn on the double-sided oven at 230 C degree
    • The oven without oil does not need to be turned on first
    • If using a pan, you should also turn on the stove to heat the pan
  3. Diced tomatoes
  4. Mixed composite

    Mix tomatoes, salt, a little olive oil (1-2 tablespoons), pepper, Balsamic vinegar, garlic as the ratio above. Once the mixture is done, set them aside.

  5. Slice the bread and pop them in the oven

    Slice the bread to a thickness of about 2-4 cm. Then spread olive oil on both sides of the bread and prepare to put it in the oven. The temperature here fluctuates between 200-210 degrees Celsius. If you decide to pan-fry, try to make the bread reach a certain golden and crispy.

  6. Mixture moves onto the bread

    This step is the last step before the dish is finished. However, I recommend that you re-steam the mixture and reduce the seasoning to make the dish best for you.
